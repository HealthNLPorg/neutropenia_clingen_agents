Mutation Syntax
Definition: The changing of the structure of a gene, resulting in a variant form, caused by the alteration of
single base units in DNA, or the deletion, insertion, or rearrangement of larger sections of genes or
chromosomes. The mutation syntax attribute is an exact specification of the mutation, either a change in the
nucleotide sequence that affects the amino acid formed or a change in the amino acid that affects the protein
formed.
