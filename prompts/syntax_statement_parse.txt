Mutation Syntax: The changing of the structure of a gene, resulting in a variant form, caused by the alteration of
single base units in DNA, or the deletion, insertion, or rearrangement of larger sections of genes or
chromosomes. The mutation syntax attribute is an exact specification of the mutation, either a change in the
nucleotide sequence that affects the amino acid formed or a change in the amino acid that affects the protein
formed.

Clinical Genomics Results Statement:
A clinical genomics results statement consists of one or more attributes and values that together provide the most basic information necessary to interpret the results of a clinical genomic test, for instance indication of variation allele frequency (VAF) and its percentage.
Statements may be of a variety of types based on the test performed, containing different entities and interpretations based on statement type.
